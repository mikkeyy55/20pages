Bicycle Safety Guidelines

Before You Ride

  • Always wear a properly fitted helmet.
  • Inspect brakes, tire pressure, chain, and lights before each ride.
  • Wear bright or reflective clothing, especially at night.
  • Use a white front light and a red rear reflector/light when riding in low visibility.

While Riding

  • Obey all traffic laws—ride with traffic, not against it.
  • Use hand signals when turning or stopping.
  • Stay in designated bike lanes or keep to the right side of the road.
  • Stay alert—watch out for cars, pedestrians, and obstacles on the road.
  • Do not wear headphones or use your phone while riding.

Protective Gear

  • Wear knee and elbow pads if you're doing tricks or riding off-road.
  • Use gloves to improve grip and protect your hands.
  • Consider wearing shatterproof glasses to shield your eyes from debris.

For Beginner Riders

  • Practice in a safe, open area until you’re comfortable with balance and braking.
  • Ride with a more experienced cyclist if you're unsure about road cycling.
  • Learn basic bike signals and traffic signs before going on public roads.
