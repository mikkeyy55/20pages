Types of Bicycles

Road Bikes

Designed for high-speed riding on paved roads, these bikes are lightweight and feature narrow tires.

Mountain Bikes

Built for rugged terrain, with thick tires and suspension systems to handle bumps and dirt paths.

Hybrid Bikes

A cross between road and mountain bikes, suitable for city riding and light trail adventures.

Gravel / Adventure Bikes

Versatile bikes built for long distances over mixed terrain like pavement, gravel, and dirt.

Electric Bikes (E-Bikes)

Provide motor-assisted pedaling, great for commuting or riders who want a boost uphill or on long trips.

Folding Bikes

Compact and foldable, ideal for commuters with limited storage space or who use public transport.

Cruiser Bikes

Comfortable and stylish, cruisers are perfect for relaxed riding around neighborhoods or beach paths.

BMX Bikes

Small, stunt-ready bikes designed for tricks, racing, or skate park performance.

Track / Fixie Bikes

Simple, single-speed bikes often used for racing in velodromes or minimalist urban riding.

Recumbent Bikes

Allow riders to sit in a laid-back reclining position for ergonomic comfort on long journeys.

Cargo Bikes

Heavy-duty bikes equipped with racks or containers for transporting goods or passengers.

Touring Bikes

Designed for long-distance travel with luggage, offering stability and comfort over long rides.

Tandem Bikes

Built for two riders, these bikes require teamwork and are ideal for recreational riding in pairs.

Fat Bikes

Feature extra-wide tires for stability and traction on sand, snow, or rough trails.

Utility Bikes

Everyday bikes designed for functionality, often including racks, baskets, and built-in lights.
