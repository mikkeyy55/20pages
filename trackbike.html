Track / Fixed-Gear Bikes

Overview

Track bikes, also known as fixed-gear or fixie bikes, are minimalist bicycles originally designed for velodrome racing. They are characterized by having a single gear with no freewheel mechanism, meaning the rider must keep pedaling while the bike is moving. Their simplicity, lightweight design, and urban aesthetic have made them popular among city riders and cycling purists.

Key Features

  • Single fixed gear (no coasting — pedals move with the wheels)
  • Lightweight frame with aerodynamic geometry
  • No derailleurs or shifters
  • Often brakeless (or front brake only) for track use
  • Minimalist, clean design favored for urban riding

Best For

Track bikes are best for smooth, flat terrain such as velodromes, city streets, and paved roads. They’re ideal for experienced riders who value control, efficiency, and a connection to the road. Fixies are especially loved by commuters and messengers in urban environments.

Advantages

  • Simple mechanics and low maintenance
  • Lightweight and fast for city riding
  • Teaches efficient pedaling and cadence control
  • Stylish, clean aesthetic with full rider control

Maintenance Tips

  • Regularly inspect the chain tension and rear cog
  • Keep the drivetrain clean and lubricated
  • Check tires and rims frequently, especially for urban wear
  • If using brakes, check pad wear and cable tension
